Lotus pose - Padmasana

Benefits of Lotus Pose: Calms the brain. Increases awareness and attentiveness. Keeps the spine straight. Helps develop good posture.

Low Lunge with a Side Stretch - Anjaneyasana

Low Lunge Pose stretches and expands the muscles of the hip and spine to better flexibility. The raising and extending the arm gives a stretching to the entire side of the body.

Pigeon Pose - Pada Rajkapotasana

Pigeon Pose helps improve the range of motion in the hips. It may also strengthen the muscles that support the hips and lower back. May also help relieve lower back pain by stretching the muscles and tendons around the spine.

Upward Facing Dog - Urdhva Mukha Svanasana

Upward-Facing Dog is an energy-boosting posture that may help relieve lower back pain. It can also help counteract slouching and kyphosis (the abnormal curvature of the spine). It is a great posture to counteract the effects of prolonged sitting.

Forearm Balance Pose - Pincha Mayurasana

Forearm Balance also called Feathered Peacock pose offers improved circulation, a calm mind, less stress, and better digestion. In this pose, keep your shoulder blades lifted and broadened while pressing your forearms, wrist, and palms into the ground for a strong, supportive base.
